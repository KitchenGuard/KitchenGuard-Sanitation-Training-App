• Material Notes: For nonstick saucepans, avoid using metal utensils or abrasive scrubbers to prevent scratching. For stainless steel, a simple paste of baking soda and water can help remove burnt or stuck food.

    1. Allow the pan to cool completely before washing—avoid putting a hot pan under cold water.
    2. Remove leftover food and gently scrape residues using a rubber or silicone spatula.
    3. Rinse with warm water to clear loose bits.
    4. If needed, fill the pan with warm soapy water and let it soak for a few minutes.
    5. Wash using a soft sponge; for tougher stains, sprinkle baking soda and scrub gently.
    6. Rinse thoroughly to remove soap and residue.
    7. Dry immediately with a clean towel to prevent water spots and rust.

  • Material Notes: For enamel-coated or nonstick stock pots, avoid using metal scrubbers to prevent scratching. For stainless-steel pots, a baking soda and water paste works well for removing burnt or stuck-on food.

    5. Wash using a soft sponge; apply baking soda for tougher stains or sticky spots.
    6. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap and residue.
    7. Dry the pot immediately with a clean cloth to avoid water stains or rust.

  • Material Notes: For nonstick Dutch ovens, avoid metal scrubbers to prevent scratching the surface. For stainless-steel Dutch ovens, a paste of baking soda and water works well to remove burnt or stuck-on food. Cast iron Dutch ovens should be hand-washed gently and dried immediately to prevent rust.

    1. Let the Dutch oven cool completely before washing—never place a hot oven in cold water.
    2. Empty leftover food and gently scrape the inside with a rubber or silicone spatula.
    3. Rinse under warm running water to remove loose debris.
    4. If there are stubborn residues, fill the oven with warm soapy water and let it soak for a few minutes.
    5. Wash with a soft sponge; for tougher stains, sprinkle a little baking soda and scrub gently.
    6. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap and residue.
    7. Dry immediately with a clean towel to prevent water spots or rust, especially on cast iron surfaces.
