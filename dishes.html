1. Scrape: Remove leftover food into the trash or compost.
    2. Rinse: Quickly rinse under warm water to loosen residues.
    3. Soak (if needed): Fill sink with warm water + a few drops of dish soap and soak for 5-15 minutes if food is stuck.
    4. Wash: Use a soft sponge or dishcloth and wash each plate inside and out. For stubborn spots, sprinkle baking soda and scrub gently.
    5. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.
    6. Dry: Either air-dry on a rack or towel-dry immediately to avoid water spots.
    7. Store: Stack gently with a thin cloth or plate separators if they're delicate.

      8. **Dishwasher note: porcelain, ceramic, and most stoneware are dishwasher safe; avoid putting thin crystal or gold-trimmed plates in the dishwasher.**

    1. Scrape: Shake off food into bin.
    2. Pre-rinse: Rinse under warm water.
    3. Wash: Use warm soapy water and scrub with a sponge or utensil brush. Pay attention to joints/hinges.
    4. Sanitize (optional): For extra hygiene, soak 1–2 minutes in a bleach solution (1 tablespoon bleach per gallon of water) or use the dishwasher’s sanitize cycle.
    5. Rinse & Dry: Rinse thoroughly and dry immediately, or place in utensil holder to air dry handle-up.
    6. Store: Keep in a dry drawer or holder.

      7. **Material tip: wood handled utensils should be hand washed only and dried promptly to avoid splitting.**

    1. Safety first: Never leave knives submerged where they can’t be seen. Handle by the spine or handle only.
    2. Rinse immediately after use to remove food particles.
    3. Wash by hand with warm water, mild dish soap, and a soft sponge — swipe from the spine to the edge (not towards your fingers). Do not soak knives for long periods.
    4. Rinse & dry immediately with a towel to prevent rust or spotting.
    5. Store safely in a knife block, magnetic strip, or knife guard.

      6. **Extra: Do not put quality knives in the dishwasher — the heat, detergent, and tumbling dull and damage blades. For carbon steel knives, wipe a thin coat of food-grade oil occasionally to prevent rust.**

    1. Cool the pan to room temperature (don’t immerse a hot pan in cold water).
    2. Dump out leftover food and scrape gently with a rubber spatula.
    3. Rinse under warm running water to remove loose bits.
    4. Add dish soap + warm water to soak if needed.

    6. Rinse thoroughly.
